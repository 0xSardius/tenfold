# ROADMAP — Daily Idea-Generation App

**Name:** _Tenfold_
**One-liner:** A daily practice app that makes you measurably better at generating ideas, built on James Altucher's "10 ideas a day" method.
**Last updated:** 2026-06-01

---

## Core thesis & non-negotiables

These hold across every phase. If a feature violates one of these, it doesn't ship.

1. **The product is a tool, not a game.** The reason to return is that it works — visible skill growth, a compounding idea archive, ideas you actually use — not points. Gamification is a thin, fade-able scaffold for the cold-start only, never the engine.
2. **The daily practice is sacred and private.** The act of writing your ten ideas is token-free, judgment-free, and private by default — forever. Social, curation, and (eventually) the token live only in an opt-in public layer. The token never touches the practice.
3. **Altucher fidelity.** Quantity over quality; suspend judgment; the value is in straining past the easy first few ideas (ideas ~5–10 are "the burn"). Start at 5/day, ramp to 10.
4. **Measure the muscle, not the idea.** Progress is shown via divergent-thinking metrics (fluency, flexibility, originality, elaboration) — never by grading whether an idea is "good." Grading reintroduces the inner critic and breaks the method.
5. **Sequencing discipline.** Each phase has one gate that must be true before the next begins. The most common way these projects die is turning the token on before the loop is proven. Don't skip ahead.

## The phases

### Phase 0 — Prove it on yourself
**What's live:** Bare practice tool only — daily list (start at 5, ramp to 10), prompt engine, private archive, AI coach reflecting muscle-metrics. No social, no token, no points.
**Who uses it:** You, the founder. Dogfood.
**Gate to advance:** You personally run it daily *through the 30–90 day dip* and come out feeling it work — metrics climbing, archive paying rent, genuine reluctance to lose it.
**Spine metric:** Your own honest answer to "would I be upset if this disappeared?"
**Main risk:** Building Phase 1 instead of actually using Phase 0.

### Phase 1 — The practice, in public
**What's live:** Web-first app that also renders as a mini app across Base App + Farcaster clients. Full onboarding ramp, prompt engine, compounding archive, AI coach, one light fade-able streak scaffold for the dip. Opt-in sharing of a *single curated idea* as a cast (the "transmitter" move = acquisition loop). Practice itself stays private.
**Unlocks:** Distribution via casts; the shared-idea cast is the viral loop.
**Gate to advance:** Retention *without a token*. A cohort visibly getting through 30–90 days, returning because it works — not because they were paid.
**Spine metric:** % of new users still doing the practice at **Day 60**.
**Main risk:** Mistaking Farcaster novelty traffic for real retention; audience ceiling (~40–60k crypto-native DAU).

### Phase 2 — The commons, run on play money
**What's live:** Public idea pool. Community can browse, curate, and vote — but the entire curation / lottery / staking system runs on **non-transferable points or reputation first**. This is the testnet for the economy: quadratic weighting, conviction voting, anti-slop bonds, lottery — all dry-run with no real money. AI begins clustering themes across the corpus (the "hyperstition pool" becomes visible).
**Unlocks:** Validated curation mechanics; emergent-theme surfacing.
**Gate to advance:** Curation visibly surfaces good ideas (signal > noise); participation dense enough that a real market wouldn't be empty; **and the points economy behaves** (if people game worthless points, real money makes it strictly worse).
**Spine metric:** Curator hit-rate — do staked/points-backed ideas outperform random on independent quality review?
**Main risk:** Empty-room problem; sybil/farming surfacing even at the points level (useful to learn here, cheaply).

### Phase 3 — Turn the token on
**What's live:** Launch the coin via Bankr. It flows through mechanics already debugged in Phase 2: staked curation, quadratically-funded community lottery, curation rewards for early backers, builder commitment bonds, stake-for-access sinks. Swap fees seed the treasury that funds the lottery pool.

**Token-readiness checklist — ALL must be true before flipping the switch:**
1. The loop retains *without* the token (Phase 1 proven) — the token amplifies, doesn't prop up.
2. A real corpus + active crowd exist (Phase 2 proven) — something for value to accrue *from*.
3. Curation mechanics already work in points form — financializing a debugged system, not a hypothesis.
4. Sinks exist, not just a faucet — stake-for-access + fees-as-sink so supply isn't only flowing out.
5. Legal/structure handled — entity, counsel, securities + tax view, treasury custody. Before, not after.
6. You've consciously decided to **become a token company** — the hard-to-reverse fork.

**Main risk:** Every token risk at once — speculation eating the mission, whale capture, regulatory exposure, contamination of the practice.
**Non-negotiable mitigation:** Daily practice stays 100% token-free; the token lives only in the commons.

### Phase 4 — The flywheel
**What's live:** The loop spins — fees → treasury → quadratic lottery → rewards to early backers → builders stake & ship → shipped ideas create narrative + volume → more fees. The corpus is now the creativity knowledge database; every idea that gets *built* is proof of the hyperstition thesis. The original "vote to fund" / idea-markets belong here.
**Gate to call it self-sustaining:** Fee revenue covers treasury outflows; builders genuinely shipping; and the north star that overrides everything — **people are measurably getting better at generating ideas, and the token is not their main reason for staying.**
**Main risk:** Long-run crowding-out — if people are here for the token, not the practice, you've won the mechanics and lost the plot.

## The branch you must choose consciously (Phase 2 → 3)

This roadmap secretly forks:

- **If you fall in love with the personal tool:** the right answer may be that the token is **never**. You run a great practice app, maybe with light *non-token* curation. That is a complete, fundable business — and arguably a better one.
- **If the real product is an idea economy:** Phase 3+ is the point, and the token is the mechanism that makes it work.

The roadmap doesn't make this choice for you. It just ensures you don't make it by accident.

## Decisions still open

- **Reflection timing:** AI coach feedback immediately on finishing (instant dopamine) vs. next morning (a reason to return). Worth A/B testing in Phase 1.
- **Bad-day rigidity:** does the app ever let you off with fewer than 10, or is the rigidity part of the medicine?
- **Mainstream vs. crypto-native:** Farcaster caps Phase 1–2 scale. If the loop proves out, decide whether to broaden to a standalone mainstream app or lean fully into the crypto-native idea economy.
